One struggle, told in several dialects.

It began with a small observation: Satan gets the movies; Saint Michael rarely does. Fear is easy to dramatize—its counterforce is usually left abstract. This tapestry gives the counterforce equal light.

Napoleon Hill names the machinery of fear and drifting. Stoicism trains the inner citadel. Saint Michael gives courage and protection a face. Literature lets chains, storms, swords, gardens, and roads carry what argument alone cannot. The throughline is not eclecticism: it is one figure learning to hold the same golden thread through diagnosis, protection, discipline, and return.
