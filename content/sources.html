Napoleon Hill

Primary text

Napoleon Hill, Outwitting the Devil: The Secret to Freedom and Success, written in 1938 and first published in 2011 by the Napoleon Hill Foundation.

Ideas used in the tapestry

  • drifting and definiteness of purpose
  • the six basic fears
  • hypnotic rhythm and the force of repeated habit
  • self-discipline, applied faith, and learning from adversity

Throughline defaults to short teaching paraphrases. Any future direct quotation should be checked against a named edition and cited at panel level.

Stoic practice

  • Epictetus, Enchiridion and Discourses—especially the distinction between what is and is not up to us.
  • Marcus Aurelius, Meditations—the ruling faculty, the inward citadel, and disciplined response to impressions.
  • Seneca, Moral Letters to Lucilius—practice under adversity, fear, and the training of judgment.

The panel language is interpretive paraphrase rather than a claim of direct quotation. Public-domain translations are preferred when a short quotation is added.

Saint Michael and scripture

  • Revelation 12:7–9—the war in heaven and Michael's battle with the dragon.
  • Daniel 10:13, 10:21, and 12:1—Michael as prince and protector.
  • Jude 1:9—Michael the archangel.
  • Ephesians 6:10–18—the armor of God.
  • The Prayer to Saint Michael, traditionally attributed to Pope Leo XIII.

Scriptural language should name its translation when quoted. The artwork is devotional-symbolic interpretation, not a claim to reproduce one historical painting or settle doctrinal debate.

Literary and symbolic echoes

  • Prudentius, Psychomachia—the inward struggle rendered as combat.
  • Dante Alighieri, Divine Comedy—lostness, guidance, descent, ascent, and a fixed ordering light.
  • John Milton, Paradise Lost—rebellion, angelic conflict, and the visual scale of spiritual choice.
  • John Bunyan, The Pilgrim's Progress—Apollyon, the burden, the road, and the Celestial City.
  • Johann Wolfgang von Goethe, Faust—temptation, bargain, and the wager over a life.

Visual provenance

The panel system and prompt bible are curated in somarc/through-line-sites. Final site media is generated with Grok Imagine through the tracked, approval-gated Riverboat workflow in this repository. The local manifest binds each approved file to its prompt, Imagine tool event, normalized output, and SHA-256 hash.

No generated plate contains authoritative text. Titles, insights, attribution, and questions remain authored HTML so they can be corrected without repainting the art.

Reading policy

This tapestry is an artful interpretation and an invitation to read. It is not a substitute for the original works, spiritual counsel, or mental-health care. Where traditions resonate, the site names the resonance; it does not claim they are identical.
